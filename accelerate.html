Census Accelerate


What is Census Accelerate?
We match creatives with community-based Census outreach efforts to generate innovative content that addresses challenges for the 2020 Census specific to hard-to-count communities.
Led by the U.S. Census Bureau, Accelerate is an open, grassroots call for content, complementing its official campaign with the works of outside designers, content strategists, storytellers, filmmakers and others. The program works through content development sprints, partnerships, and events to bring together creatives, technologists, civic leaders and community organizers to ensure everyone in our country, regardless of location, income, race or ethnicity, is represented in the 2020 Census.
The U.S. Census
What is the Decennial Census?

The Decennial Census is a Constitutionally-mandated count of every person living in the United States, once each decade. This population count is used to determine apportionment (the number of seats each state gets in the U.S. House of Representatives) and how to distribute over $675 billion of annual federal funds to states, cities, and local communities for critical infrastructure and services such as housing, highways, schools, hospitals, and more.

For the next Decennial Census, which begins in the Spring of 2020, every U.S. resident will be able to complete the Census online. (Phone and mail options are also available. If a household does not respond, an enumerator will take the Census at their doorstep.) This Census will count an increasingly diverse and growing population of about 330 million people in more than 140 million housing units, presenting new challenges.


Challenges to a complete and accurate count

Historically, certain groups have been undercounted at disproportionately high rates, including millennials, rural communities, families with young children, immigrants, non-native English speakers, renters, underrepresented minorities, and people experiencing homelessness - these are called "hard-to-count" communities. If these groups aren't counted, their communities may not receive their fair share of countless services, resources, and representation. It also impacts the data upon which countless policy, legal, and business decisions are made.

Get Involved
How can people and organizations get involved?
Create-a-thons
Join one of the many #CreativesForTheCount events across cities and communities across the U.S. in 2019 and 2020 to rapidly generate targeted media for hard-to-count groups. At these create-a-thons, creators and organizations of all kinds come together in person for just a few hours to generate compelling media that spread Census awareness and inoculate against disinformation.
Content Development Partnerships
Lead an organizational or brand production partnership in your region or nationally. Individuals and organizations can work independently and at their own pace to create content. To support this work, we provide you with target audience prompts, messaging best practices, and facilitate intra-organizational partnerships among collaborators.
Outreach Campaigns

Use your personal or organizational influence to get the word out to our nation's most vulnerable communities about the 2020 Census.

Influencers can draw from media generated by #CreativesForTheCount and collaborate with brilliant partners to get the content that works best for their audience, regionally or nationally. Distribution can be done in conjunction with our organized content pushes or by designing your own content rollout.
